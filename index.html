2024
A memory. A person. A June.

There are years you leave like you're closing a tab you don't need anymore.
Quietly. Unceremoniously. Just gone.
They turn into timestamps on old photos and passwords you barely remember setting.
archive / 002 — the pulse
And then there's 2024.
I miss 2024 in a way that makes me feel stupid when I say it out loud. It was messy and unfinished, full of days that didn't even feel special while they were happening.

But it had a pulse.

archive / 003 — memory
The present doesn't stick.
Ask me what I ate yesterday and I have to guess.
Ask me what I did two weekends ago and I'll give you a vague outline like I'm describing someone else's life.

But 2024?

I remember 2024 with unfair clarity. Like my brain decided that year was worth saving in high resolution and everything after it can be stored as a blur.
June
archive / 004 — june 2024
That month shows up in my head like a location I can still navigate without a map.
I remember the heat that clung to my clothes. The way the nights felt heavy but alive, like the city was still awake even when it should've been asleep.

I remember how my days were a mess, and still, somehow, I had this thread running through it.

You.
archive / 005 — the dream
In the dream, everything is fine.
You're there like it's normal. Like I didn't lose you. Sometimes you don't even say anything. You're just in the passenger seat, half-lit by streetlights, legs tucked in, looking out the window like you own the night.

And the worst part is how my body believes it.

I wake up with that leftover warmth in my chest, and for half a second I think: oh. Right. We're still here.

Then the room comes back. The ceiling. The silence. The actual year.
archive / 006 — the doors
My mind makes doors.
What if I said the thing I didn't say.
What if I stayed.
What if I left earlier.
What if I didn't let it rot into silence.
What if I was braver when it mattered.
Because I can't actually open any of those doors, my brain starts building entire alternate lives behind them.
archive / 007 — the drive
Still in the car.
Still waiting at red lights
that never change.
We'd circle the same streets like we were trying to wear a groove into them. Windows down, playlists looping, the kind of conversation you only have at 1 or 2 a.m. when nobody's performing.

Just talking. Just existing next to each other.

That quiet kind of closeness that doesn't announce itself as important until it's gone.

I didn't know I was building a memory that would later ruin my sleep.

That's what time does. It doesn't warn you. It doesn't tap you on the shoulder and say, hey, pay attention, you're going to miss this so badly it'll make you reckless. It just lets you live it like it's ordinary, then takes it away and acts like you should be fine.
archive / 008 — what remains
Until then, 2024 stays. As a person. As June. As a car moving through the city too late at night. As a dream I keep having like my brain doesn't know when to let go.
I just want to wake up one day
and have the present stick again.
